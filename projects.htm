Ethical RF Hacking Educational Demo (GTRI Internship)

Objective: Create educational demo to teach about RF communications and ethical applications of RF hacking techniques

Tools & Components: HackRF Portapack, RC Cars, Dipole Antennas, Handheld UHF/VHF radios

Skills Used: spectral analysis, reverse engineering, RF signal capture and playback, use of various signal modulation techniques

Summary: During my internship at Georgia Tech Research Institute, I worked with RF hacking devices such as the Flipper Zero and the HackRF Portapack to learn about RF hacking and ways that RF hacking techniques can be leveraged effecively. During the course of the summer internship I, as well as the other interns in my group, worked to earn our HAM Radio licenses, and in the process learned much about RF communications systems and the laws surrounding them. Following this our group worked mostly with the HackRF Portapack to find a way to interfere with and furthermore take control of RF controlled RC vehicles. Using our experience we created a demo-kit which could be used to demonstrate the abilities of RF and principles of RF hacking to younger audiences.
